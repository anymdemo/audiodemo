Emotion and Intensity Regulated Text-to-Speech Synthesis Through Prompt Guidance



Abstract

Achieving natural, emotionally expressive speech synthesis remains challenging due to the scarcity of high-quality annotations and the intricate interplay among prosodic, emotional, and linguistic features. In this work, we propose a novel framework that extends FastSpeech2 by integrating prompt-based prosody control with dual HuBERT encoders for robust emotion and intensity extraction. By harnessing GPT-4â€“driven prompts, our model dynamically adjusts pitch, duration, and energy at both the utterance and word levels. Objective evaluations reveal that incorporating local-level prompt control significantly enhances emotion classification accuracy, underscoring the efficacy of our approach. Furthermore, a speaker-specific ranking function refines intensity predictions, reducing reliance on scarce labels and manual heuristics.
